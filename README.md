## About This Working Group

The Community Management Working Group is a domain-focused space within the Everything Open Source community for contributors interested in 
the human side of open source — building communities, supporting contributors, managing engagement, and creating spaces where people 
feel welcomed, valued, and empowered to contribute.

We exist to:

- Create a dedicated space for community builders and managers to connect, learn, and collaborate
- Document processes, frameworks, and best practices for managing open-source communities effectively
- Support contributors who want to grow their skills in community management
- Lay the foundation for the Everything Open Source Working Group ecosystem by piloting the Working Group model in 2026
- Build a body of knowledge and a set of reusable resources that will help launch and sustain new Working Groups across other domains 
  in 2027 and beyond

This Working Group is also about building the Everything Open Source community itself. 
The work we do here directly shapes how contributors experience open source across Africa and beyond.

---

## Who This Is For

This Working Group is open to anyone interested in community management and the human side of open source, including:

- Community managers and community builders
- Developer advocates and developer relations professionals
- Contributors interested in onboarding, engagement, and contributor experience
- Open-source project maintainers who want to build healthier communities around their projects
- Students and early-career professionals exploring community management as a career path
- Anyone who believes that open source is about people first and code second
- Complete beginners who want to learn what community management in open source actually looks like

You do not need a title or formal experience to join. If you care about people and want to help build something meaningful, you belong here.

## What We Work On

The Community Management Working Group focuses on the following areas:

**Building and sustaining open-source communities**
We explore what makes open-source communities healthy, welcoming, and sustainable, and we document what we learn so others can benefit from it.

**Contributor onboarding and experience**
We develop and improve resources, processes, and frameworks that help new contributors find their footing in open source quickly and confidently.

**Community engagement and retention**
We explore strategies for keeping contributors engaged, recognized, and motivated beyond their first contribution.

**Working Group development**
A major focus for 2026 is building and documenting the Everything Open Source Working Group model so that it can be replicated across new domains in 2027. Through this Working Group, we are designing the blueprint for how all Everything Open Source Working Groups will operate.

**Community management as a career**
We create resources and conversations that support people who want to pursue community management and developer relations as a professional path.

## Our 2026 Goals

2026 is a foundational year for the Everything Open Source Working Group ecosystem. The Community Management Working Group is the first Working Group we are launching, and one of our most important responsibilities this year is to prove the model works.

Here is what we are working toward in 2026:

**Build a thriving, active Working Group**
- Establish a consistent meeting cadence and maintain it throughout the year
- Grow Working Group membership to *(add target number)* active members
- Publish meeting notes for every session without exception
- Celebrate and recognize Working Group contributors regularly

**Document the Working Group model**
- Build a comprehensive Working Group blueprint that captures everything we learn about running an effective Working Group at Everything Open Source
- Create reusable templates, frameworks, and guides that can be used to launch new Working Groups in 2027
- Document what works, what does not, and what we would do differently so future Working Group leads have a head start

**Build a community management resource library**
- Curate and publish a library of high-quality community management resources relevant to open-source communities
- Produce original content including guides, playbooks, and frameworks that reflect our own experience and learning
- Make all resources freely accessible in this repository

**Support Everything Open Source sprints**
- Actively participate in Everything Open Source sprints and represent community management as a meaningful contribution domain
- Support sprint participants who want to contribute through community management rather than code
- Document community management contributions at each sprint and share them publicly

## How to Contribute

There are many ways to contribute to this Working Group beyond attending meetings:

- **Share resources:**
Found a great article, podcast, course, or framework relevant to community management in open source? Open an issue using the 
[Resource Suggestion template](../../issues/new/choose) and share it with the group.

- **Write or improve documentation:**
Help us build out our playbooks, guides, and templates. Open a pull request with your additions or improvements.

**Share your experience:**
Have you managed or built a community before? Share your experience, lessons learned, and frameworks with the Working Group. Even a short write-up or a discussion thread can be enormously valuable to others.

**Lead or co-lead a meeting:**
Volunteer to facilitate an upcoming meeting. Let the Working Group leads know in Discord or open a discussion.

**Propose and lead a project:**
Have an idea for something the Working Group should build, document, or explore? Open a [Project Proposal issue](../../issues/new/choose) and share your thinking.

**Mentor a newcomer:**
If you have experience in community management, offer to support someone newer to the field. A conversation, a resource 
recommendation, or a quick introduction can open doors for someone just starting out.

**Help build the 2027 Working Group roadmap:**
One of our most important projects this year is laying the groundwork for new Working Groups launching in 2027. If you have ideas about which domains should be prioritized or want to help plan the expansion, get involved in this project.

**Contribute at a sprint:**
Everything Open Source runs regular sprints where contributors work alongside open-source project maintainers. Community management is a meaningful sprint contribution domain and this Working Group actively supports sprint participants who want to contribute in this area.
[Learn more about sprints →](https://github.com/EverythingOpenSource/community#programs)

Please read our [CONTRIBUTING.md](./CONTRIBUTING.md) before making your first contribution.
