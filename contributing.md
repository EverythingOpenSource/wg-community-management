## Before You Contribute

Before making your first contribution, please take a few minutes to:

1. **Read the README** — familiarize yourself with the Working Group's focus, goals, and structure [README.md](./README.md)

2. **Read the Code of Conduct** — all contributions and interactions in this Working Group are governed by our Code of Conduct
   [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)

3. **Introduce yourself** — open a GitHub Discussion using the [Introduction template](../../issues/new/choose) and let the community know you are here

4. **Join our Discord** — connect with Working Group members in real time at *(add Discord channel link)*

5. **Look around** — browse open issues, active discussions, and the resources folder to get a feel for what the Working Group is working on

## Ways to Contribute

You do not need to write code to contribute meaningfully to this Working Group. Here are the ways you can get involved:

---

### Participate in Discussions

One of the most valuable things you can do is show up and engage. Join conversations in GitHub Discussions, share your perspective, 
ask questions, and respond to others.

**How to do it:**
- Visit the [Discussions tab](../../discussions)
- Join an existing conversation or start a new one
- Use the appropriate discussion category for your post

---

### Attend and Contribute to Meetings

Our Working Group meets regularly and everyone is welcome. You do not need to be an expert to attend. Showing up, listening, and 
contributing your perspective is enough.

**How to do it:**
- Check the meeting schedule in the README
- Add agenda items by opening a 
  [Meeting Agenda issue](../../issues/new/choose) before the meeting
- Attend the meeting using the link in the README
- Volunteer to take notes or facilitate a session

---

### Add to the Resource Library

Our resource library is a living collection of tools, articles, courses, frameworks, and communities relevant to open-source 
community management. Anyone can suggest a resource.

**How to do it:**
- Open a new issue using the [Resource Suggestion template](../../issues/new/choose)
- Fill in all the relevant details about the resource
- A Working Group lead will review and add it to the library

If you want to add the resource directly yourself:
1. Fork this repository
2. Navigate to the [`resources/`](./resources/) folder
3. Add your resource to the relevant file in the correct format 
   (see [Resource Format Guidelines](#resource-format-guidelines) below)
4. Open a pull request with a clear description of what you added

---

### Write or Improve Documentation

Good documentation is at the heart of what we do in this Working Group. If you spot something that is unclear, outdated, or missing, please 
fix it or flag it.

**Types of documentation you can contribute to:**
- README updates and improvements
- Resource library entries
- Meeting notes
- Playbooks and frameworks in the [`projects/`](./projects/) folder
- Templates in the [`templates/`](./templates/) folder
- Onboarding guides and contributor documentation

**How to do it:**
1. Fork this repository
2. Create a new branch for your changes
3. Make your edits
4. Open a pull request with a clear description of what you changed 
   and why

---

### Propose a Project

Have an idea for something the Working Group should build, research, 
or document? Bring it to the community.

**How to do it:**
- Open an issue using the 
  [Project Proposal template](../../issues/new/choose)
- Fill in the details including what the project is, why it 
  matters, what it will produce, and whether you are willing 
  to lead it
- The Working Group leads will review the proposal at the next meeting 
  and follow up in the issue

---

### Lead or Facilitate a Meeting

If you want to take on more responsibility in the Working Group, volunteering to facilitate a meeting is a great first step. 
Facilitators keep the meeting on track, make sure everyone has a chance to contribute, and help close out action items clearly.

**How to do it:**
- Let the Working Group leads know in Discord or comment on the relevant meeting agenda issue
- Review the meeting agenda at least 24 hours before the session
- Use the meeting notes template to document the session as you go or immediately after

---

### Mentor a Newcomer

If you have experience in community management, offer to support someone newer to the field. You can do this informally through 
Discord or by pairing on a specific project or contribution.

**How to do it:**
- Watch for new member introduction issues and reach out directly
- Offer to pair on a resource, document, or project
- Share your experience openly in meetings and discussions

---

### Contribute at a Sprint

Everything Open Source runs regular open-source sprints where contributors work alongside project maintainers. Community 
management is a meaningful contribution domain at every sprint.

**How community managers contribute at sprints:**
- Helping draft or improve contributor onboarding documentation for sprint projects
- Writing or improving README files for participating projects
- Supporting maintainers in setting up beginner-friendly issues
- Facilitating conversations between contributors and maintainers
- Documenting the sprint experience for the Everything Open Source community

**How to get involved:**
- Watch the [Everything Open Source community repository](https://github.com/EverythingOpenSource/community) for sprint announcements
- Let the Working Group leads know you are interested in contributing at the next sprint

## Contribution Guidelines

To keep this repository clean, consistent, and welcoming, please follow these guidelines for all contributions:

**Be kind and respectful**
All contributions happen within the spirit of our [Code of Conduct](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md). Be constructive, 
patient, and generous in how you engage with others.

**Keep contributions focused**
Each pull request or issue should address one clear thing. Avoid bundling multiple unrelated changes into a single 
contribution as it makes reviewing harder.

**Use the issue templates**
When opening an issue, always use the relevant template. Templates exist to make sure contributions have the right 
context and can be reviewed efficiently.

**Check for existing work**
Before opening an issue or starting a contribution, check whether someone else is already working on the same thing. 
Browse open issues and pull requests first.

**Write clearly**
Write in plain, clear, and accessible language. Avoid jargon where possible and explain things as if the reader is encountering the topic for the first time.

**Give credit**
If your contribution builds on someone else's work, idea, or resource, acknowledge them clearly.

---

**Before opening a pull request:**
- Make sure there is an issue related to your contribution or open one first
- Check that your changes follow the writing style guide below
- Review your contribution for spelling, grammar, and clarity

## Recognition and Credit

Every contribution to this workgroup is valued and recognized. 
Here is how we celebrate contributors:

- **Contributor shoutouts** in meeting notes and Discord 
  after significant contributions
- **Workgroup contributor spotlights** shared on Everything 
  Open Source social media channels
- **Credit in documents** — if you write or significantly 
  improve a document, your name and GitHub handle will be 
  noted in the document
- **Recognition in the Everything Open Source community** — 
  outstanding workgroup contributors are highlighted in 
  community-wide updates and the annual community report

No contribution is too small to be recognized. Showing up 
consistently, asking good questions, and supporting others 
are contributions worth celebrating just as much as a 
merged pull request.

**In the repository:**

**In Discord:**
- Post your question in the workgroup Discord channel at 
  *(add link)*
- Reach out directly to a workgroup lead

**Via email:**
- Contact the Everything Open Source core team at

and get unstuck than stay quiet and feel lost. This community 
exists to support you. 💚
